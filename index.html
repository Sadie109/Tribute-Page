Betty White

The Life Of Betty White

  • Born in Oak Park, Illinois, on January 17th 1922.
  • She was an only child to Horace and Tess White, moving to Los Angeles with her family when she was 2.
  • In the early 1950's she launched her first television series Life with Elizabeth.
  • Betty continued her work in television appearing on shows The United States Steel Hour and Petticoat Junction
  • She also starred in talk shows, where is 1961 she met her third husband Allen Ludden.
  • Betty starred in the popular and well-known sitcom series The Golden Girls in the 1980's, which was among the top ranked shows during it's seven seasons and won numerous awards including an Emmy award for Betty.
  • After The Golden Girls went off air in 1992, Betty appeared on the spin off series Golden Palace
  • in 1996 she played herself on The John Larroquette Show which in turn won her another Emmy award.
  • In 2010 Betty's career caught fire again, where she appeared in a candy bar ad for the Super Bowl.
  • Due to a FB campaign Betty ended up hosting the Saturday Night Live talk show
  • From 2012 - 2017 she hosted a show called Betty White's Off Their Rockers which earned Betty an Emmy Award nomination.
  • In August 2018 PBS aired Betty White: First Lady of Television celebrating her work through-out the years in television.
  • Another passion of Betty White is her work with animals, she spent more than four decades working with the Los Angeles Zoo and the Morris Animal Foundation.
  • Living a meaningful, kind and loving life Betty White passed away peacefully at the age of 99 on December 31st 2021.
